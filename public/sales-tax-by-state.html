Sales Tax Rates by State (July 2026)

As of July 2026, statewide sales tax rates range from 0% in the five states with no sales tax to 7.25% in California, and combined state-plus-local rates run from 0% up to a 10.13% average in Louisiana. The table below lists the state rate and the average local rate for all 50 states and the District of Columbia. To apply any of these rates to a real purchase, use the free sales tax calculator.

The short version: your real rate is the state rate plus whatever your county, city, and special districts add. Five states charge no statewide sales tax, Louisiana has the highest average combined rate at 10.13%, and among states that do tax sales, Alaska's 1.82% average is the lowest.

The five states with no sales tax

Five states levy no statewide sales tax, remembered by the acronym NOMAD: New Hampshire, Oregon, Montana, Alaska, and Delaware. Four of them (New Hampshire, Oregon, Montana, and Delaware) allow no local sales taxes either, so the price on the tag is exactly what you pay. Alaska is the exception: it has no statewide tax, but its cities and boroughs levy local sales taxes that average 1.82%, so a typical Alaska purchase is still taxed. You can confirm any of these on the calculator by selecting the state.

The highest combined rates

By average combined rate, Louisiana leads at 10.13% (a 5% state rate plus 5.13% average local), followed by Tennessee at 9.61%, Washington at 9.57%, Arkansas at 9.48%, and Alabama at 9.46%. In several of these states the local layer rivals or exceeds the state layer: Alabama's 4% state rate carries an average 5.46% in local add-ons. To see the tax on a specific amount in any of these states, enter it in the calculator.

The lowest combined rates

Setting aside the four NOMAD states that charge nothing at all (Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire, and Oregon), the lowest average combined rate among states that do tax sales is Alaska at 1.82%, which comes entirely from local taxes. Hawaii is next at 4.5%, then Wyoming at 5.39%, Maine at 5.5%, and Wisconsin at 5.72%. Check any of them in the calculator to see the exact tax on a purchase.
